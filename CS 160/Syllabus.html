CS 160 Course Info

Course Description: Examines foundational computing subjects used in Computer Science and Information Technology. Topics include computer architecture, electronic logic, data representation, networking, algorithms and programming, which are used in successive Computer Science courses. Information about degrees in Computer Science and Information Technology is also covered.(4 credits)

Course Objectives: Upon successful completion of this course, each student should be able to:

1. identify specific topics of study required for various disciplines in computer science;
2. discuss social and ethical implications in computer science;
3. identify basic computer hardware components and explain their purposes;
4. describe how data is stored in electronic format and mediums;
5. explain basic TCP/IP operation and configuration in computer networks;
6. explain the significance of binary and hexadecimal in the computing field;
7. convert values between the base-2, base-10, and base-16 numbering systems;
8. explain the significance of algorithms to all programming languages;
9. create basic algorithms and solve simple programming problems;
10. use software applications required for success in subsequent Computer Science courses;
11. identify the worth of various computer industry certifications;
12. access online resources and tutorials to prepare for these certifications;
13. produce a course of study that leads to a degree.

Prerequisites: None.

The class is a combination of theory and practice. The student will use lecture material, online resources, homework assignments, quizzes, and interaction with the instructor and fellow students to gain insight into the topics covered. Because of the collaborative nature of the class and the fact that there is not a textbook, regularly attending class is essential.

Assignments: There will be numerous assignments given throughout the term. These assignments, ideally, will be done independently, but you may discuss the assignment with other class members to get clarification and/or assistance. However, keep in mind the goal of the class is to learn, so make sure you fully understand everything you submit. If you simply copy someone else's work, both you and the person from whom you copied will receive a zero on the assignment. Partial credit will be given at the instructor's discretion. Due dates and times will be posted on the course website, but assignments are generally due one week after assigned.

Quizzes: Four quizzes will be given throughout the term. Each quiz will cover the course material covered since the last quiz. You will be allowed one 8.5" x 11" sheet of self-generated notes (front and back) for each quiz.

Group Project: A term-long group project will be assigned. Details will be discussed in class.

Grading scale: 90%-100% = A, 80%-89.9% = B, 70%-79.9% = C, 60%-69.9% = D, < 60% = F

Miscellaneous

Attendance: Regular attendance is essential to your success in this course. Students are responsible for everything that takes place during class, whether covered in written materials or not. Additionally, some of each class day will be devoted to hand-on activities, and if a class must be missed, there will not be time to make up those activities in the future.

Professionalism: All participants in the class will are required to conduct themselves in a courteous, professional manner at all times. This includes communications in person, via phone, via e-mail or through an online chat or discussion.

Ethics: Plagiarism or cheating of any variety will be dealt with in accordance with the policies expressed in the student handbook. Possible consequences for academic dishonesty are: receiving no credit for the assignment in question, failing the course, and/or appearing before the college disciplinary board.

Food / Drink Policy: Eating is not allowed while class is in session. Drinking beverages is acceptable provided the beverage is in a sealable container and you aren't interfering with the learning environment of the class.

Cell Phones: Cell phones should not interfere with the learning environment of the class. If they do, the instructor will ask you to turn off your cell phone or leave class.

Special Needs: If you are a student with a disability and you believe that your opportunities to be successful in this class are affected by that disability, please contact the Clackamas CC Disability Resource Center. Their website is http://www.clackamas.edu/DisabilityResourceCenter/.

Basic Needs Security: Any student who has difficulty affording groceries or accessing sufficient food to eat every day or who lacks a safe and stable place to live, and believes this may affect their performance in the course, is urged to contact the Counseling Office at Clackamas Community College, 503.594.3176 or counseling@clackamas.edu.   Furthermore, please notify me if you are comfortable in doing so. This will enable me to provide any other resources that I may possess.

Incomplete Grades: An incomplete grade can be given at the discretion of the instructor if you have satisfactorily (C or better) completed at least 75% of the class work. Arrangements must be made with the instructor before finals week in order to be considered for an incomplete.

Emergency Procedures: When a fire alarm sounds, all students are expected to exit the building immediately. The security of items left behind is not guaranteed, however, so students should gather all personal belongings before proceeding to the nearest exit.
